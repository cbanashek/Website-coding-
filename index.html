Who was Claude Monet?

Claude Monet was a famous French painter, born December 5th 1840 and raised in Rue Laffitte, France. He died at the age of 87 in 1926 in Giverny, France. And throughout his lifetime Claude Monet, has created many art pieces that we still see today in many museums. He has created paintings that were and still are priceless.

The Water Lily Pond

The Water Lily Pond is one of Claude Monets most famous paintings. He painted it in 1899. This painting is one of a series of paitnings just like it, Monet called it Water Lilies. As well, this painting has been in the Metropolitan Museum of Art, The National Gallery since 1929.

As well, to know a little bit more about the paintng here is a quote from , "The Water Lily Pond captures Monet’s view of his Japanese bridge. While Monet would paint this bridge at various points in time- it can be seen through the blurry lenses of his cataracts in Japanese Bridge– in this version, it is shown bathed in golden light, with a calming color palette of greens and pale pinks.
